Enchiladas Verde

A simple but flavorful dish. Enchiladas Verde packs in a ton of flavor while also giving you the nostalgia of abuela's home cooking. As with a lot of traditional mexican dishes Enchiladas Verde is no different, you'll find corn tortillas along side 'Ol reliable chicken and Cojita cheese. As well as a few other ingredients (which will be listed down below).

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/4 pounds small green tomatillos *husks removed*
  • 3 serrano peppers
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 cup vegetable oil for frying
  • 9 corn tortillas
  • 3 cups of water
  • 4 teaspoons chicken bouillon granules
  • 1/2 store-bought rotisserie chicken removed and shredded
  • 1/4 head iceberg lettuce, shredded
  • 1 cup cilantro leaves
  • 1 (8 ounce) container Mexican crema: Crema Fresca
  • 1 cup grated Cotija cheese

Steps:

  1. Cover a large griddle with aluminum foil and preheat to medium high
  2. Cook tomatillos, serrano peppers, and garlic on the griddle (turning occasionally until toasted and blackened. (5 minutes for garlic, 10 for peppers, and 15 minutes for tomatillos)
  3. Remove ingredients and allow to cool
  4. Heat oil in a small, deep skillet to 350Â°F
  5. Lightly fry tortillas, one at a time in hot oil until warmed. 3 to 5 seconds per side. Drain on a paper towel-lined plate.
  6. Place toasted tomatillos, serrano peppers, and garlic in a blender. Add water and blend until smooth.
  7. Pour mixture into the saucepan over medium heat and bring to a boil. Add and mix chicken bouillon, reduce heat, and cook at a simmer until slightly thickened. (about 10 minutes).
  8. Soak tortillas in sauce, one at a time, for a few seconds.
  9. Fill each tortilla with shredded chicken and sprinkle with sauce. Roll up the tortillas and place seam-side down in a serving dish.
  10. Spoon a generous amount of sauce over the rolled tortillas, top with lettuce, cilantro, crema, and cotija cheese.
  11. Pour the remaining sauce on the top or serve on the side. Enjoy!
